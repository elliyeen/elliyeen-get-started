# Elliyeen Research — Universal Website Audit
**Date:** June 21, 2026
**Audited by:** 18-Agent Multidisciplinary Team
**Surface Product:** AI-powered revenue engineering consulting
**True Product:** The certainty that someone qualified is diagnosing why your business is not growing — and fixing it.

---

## Executive Summary — Top 10 Findings

1. **The headline does not say anything.** "Engineer Higher Profit" is a phrase, not a promise. It tells the visitor nothing about who this is for, what problem it solves, or why this company should be trusted with the job.

2. **The cycling word is a design trick that hides a positioning failure.** Cycling through Profit / Revenue / Customer Satisfaction signals the company has not decided what it leads with. Visitors do not wait for the cycle. They decide in 3 seconds.

3. **Zero proof anywhere on the page.** Case study metrics read: "More clarity. Better trust. Cleaner path." These are adjectives. A consulting company that engineers revenue needs revenue numbers. There are none.

4. **The AI chat section is a mock-up selling the idea of AI, not a tool that demonstrates it.** Every prompt is pre-selected. Every answer is static. Sophisticated buyers will see through this immediately.

5. **No founder. No face. No name. No accountability.** The footer links to a LinkedIn. The site never mentions a human being. Consulting is bought on trust in people. There is no person here.

6. **Nav labels do not match what those sections contain.** "Enterprise" links to `#cases`. "Pricing" links to `#assessment`. Visitors clicking Pricing expect to see prices.

7. **The ideal customer is undefined.** "Growth-focused organizations" is not a customer. It describes approximately 100% of all businesses. There is no specificity about who this is for, what size, what stage, what industry.

8. **$2.4M+ revenue opportunity is unearned.** The number appears before the visitor has entered any information. It signals nothing. It is a decoration.

9. **Case studies have no outcomes.** Three client stories. Zero numbers. Zero quotes. Zero names. The Savannah Personal Care case — for a home care agency — says "More clarity." That is not a result.

10. **The site describes what Elliyeen does. It never shows what the client's life looks like after working with Elliyeen.** The future state — the reason to act — is never painted.

## Agent 10 — Story Audit

**Current section sequence:**
1. Hero (Engineer Higher [word])
2. AI Advisor demo
3. Process (4 steps)
4. Case Studies
5. CTA block
6. Footer

**Story assessment:** This is an information stack, not a story. The sequence does not follow a narrative arc. There is no Act 1 (the problem), no Act 2 (the stakes of inaction), no Act 3 (the transformation). The site opens with a solution and closes with a repeat of the same solution. The visitor never feels understood.

**Is the customer the hero?** No. The site talks about Elliyeen's capabilities. The customer's situation, fear, and desired outcome are never named. "Free your organization to focus on what matters most" is company-centric language dressed up to sound customer-centric.

**The Missing Scene:** What does the client's life look like 90 days after working with Elliyeen?

> "Three months ago, your website was processing visitors without converting them. You knew something was wrong but couldn't see what. Today, you know exactly where every dollar of friction was hiding. The intake form that buried your call-to-action. The FAQ that answered no question your customers actually asked. The case studies with no numbers. All of it identified, prioritized, and fixed. Your conversion rate is climbing. You know why."

This scene does not exist anywhere on the site. It should be the emotional peak before the final CTA.

**Recommended section sequence:**
1. Hero — specific customer, specific problem, specific promise
2. Problem section — mirror the internal monologue of the buyer (name the fear)
3. The future state — paint what life looks like after the audit
4. How it works — process with specificity (timelines, deliverables, 18 frameworks named)
5. Proof — one case study with real numbers, one testimonial with a real name
6. Founder section — who does this work and why should I trust them
7. Objection FAQ — price, timeline, what happens next
8. CTA — specific, low-friction, earned by everything above

**Story Score: 3/10**
