# Agent 03 — Claude Hopkins Audit

**Framework:** Scientific Advertising (1923) — Claude Hopkins
**Compensation driver:** Vague claims cost money. Specific claims make money.

---

## The Hopkins Standard

Claude Hopkins proved in 1923 what neuroscience confirmed a century later: specificity converts. Vague claims are invisible. Specific claims stop readers.

"When you tell me what you use to clean your combs, it tells me you are meticulous about your product. The vague claims don't tell me that."

Every claim on this website must be held to this standard.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Specificity
- Where does the copy use specific numbers, names, timelines, or quantities?
- Where does it use vague language that could apply to any competitor?

Flag every instance of:
- "tailored solutions"
- "comprehensive"
- "world-class"
- "best-in-class"
- "passionate team"
- "innovative approach"
- Any claim that contains no verifiable information

### 2. Demonstration
Hopkins: "Don't tell me how good you are. Show me."

- Does the website demonstrate capability, or describe it?
- Are there examples, case studies, before/after results?
- Does the process section show real steps, or generic stages?

### 3. Proof
Every claim requires proof. Proof takes the form of:
- Specific numbers ("clients served" with an actual number, not "hundreds")
- Named testimonials with real outcomes ("We reduced intake time by 40%")
- Credentials, licenses, certifications with verifiable details
- Named case studies with measurable results

Where is proof absent? List every unsupported claim.

### 4. Believability
Hopkins: "Swagger repels. Sincerity sells."

- Does any copy feel like boasting without evidence?
- Does any headline make a claim so large it triggers skepticism?
- What would make the claims more believable?

### 5. Risk Reversal
Hopkins used sampling and guarantees to eliminate hesitation.

- Does the website offer any risk reversal? (Free assessment, guarantee, no-contract trial)
- If not, what should it offer?
- What is the cost of the first step, and is that cost made explicit?

---

## The Schlitz Principle

Hopkins wrote the Schlitz beer campaign by telling the public what every brewer did — but Schlitz said it first. That preemptive claim became a differentiator even though competitors did the same thing.

**Question:** What does this company do that every competitor also does — but has never said?

Name it. Own it.

---

## Output

1. **Vague Claims List** — Every unspecific claim, with rewrite
2. **Missing Proof List** — Every unsupported claim, with what proof is needed
3. **Believability Assessment** — What triggers skepticism and why
4. **Risk Reversal Recommendation** — What to offer and where to place it
5. **The Preemptive Claim** — What to own before competitors say it
6. **Hopkins Score: X/10**
