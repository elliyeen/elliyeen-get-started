# Agent 02 — Positioning Audit

**Frameworks:** McKinsey Digital · David Ogilvy · Joanna Wiebe
**Compensation driver:** Weak positioning means the company competes on price. Strong positioning makes price irrelevant.

---

## Mission

Determine whether the website communicates a position that is clear, credible, and differentiated — or whether it sounds like everyone else in the category.

---

## Core Questions

1. **Who is the ideal customer?**
   - Age, role, situation, emotional state at the moment they land on the page.
   - Is this customer described anywhere on the website, or is the site written for everyone?

2. **What problem are they trying to solve?**
   - Not the surface problem. The underlying fear, frustration, or desire.
   - Does the website name that problem, or describe features instead?

3. **What makes this company different?**
   - One sentence. No jargon. No "tailored solutions."
   - If the sentence could appear on a competitor's site, it is not a differentiator.

4. **Does the website communicate that difference?**
   - Where does the differentiator appear? Hero? Buried below the fold?
   - Is it stated explicitly, or implied and easy to miss?

5. **What is the competitive moat?**
   - What can this company say that no competitor can say?
   - Is that claim present, prominent, and provable?

## Ogilvy Test

David Ogilvy: "Unless your advertising contains a big idea, it will pass like a ship in the night."

Answer: What is the Big Idea on this website?

If you cannot identify it in one sentence, the positioning is failing.

---

## Competitive Frame

Name the top 3 competitors in this category.

For each, write their implied positioning in one sentence.

Then write the audited company's implied positioning in one sentence.

Identify the gap. That gap is either an opportunity or evidence of sameness.

---

## Output

1. Ideal customer profile (as written, vs. as it should be)
2. Stated problem (as written, vs. actual customer fear)
3. Differentiator (as written, vs. what it should be)
4. Positioning score table
5. The Big Idea — exists or missing
6. Competitive frame analysis
7. Recommended positioning statement (one sentence, no jargon)
