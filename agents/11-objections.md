# Agent 11 — Objection Audit

**Frameworks:** Joanna Wiebe (Copy Hackers) · CXL Institute · Hopkins
**Compensation driver:** Every unanswered objection is a closed tab.

---

## Mission

Identify every objection a qualified prospect holds when they arrive on this website, and determine how many of them the website answers before the visitor has to ask.

A prospect who has to ask an objection in order to get it answered is already gone.

---

## The Universal Objection Framework

Every prospect, in every category, holds some version of these objections:

### 1. Price
- "Is this going to be too expensive?"
- "Am I getting value relative to what I'm paying?"
- "Is there a cheaper alternative?"
- "Will I be surprised by hidden costs?"

**Is this addressed? Where? How well?**

### 2. Trust
- "How do I know this company will deliver what they promise?"
- "Who else has used them and what happened?"
- "What happens if things go wrong?"
- "Is this real, or is it marketing?"

**Is this addressed? Where? How well?**

### 3. Fit
- "Is this actually for someone in my situation?"
- "Do they understand my specific problem, or is this generic?"
- "Am I the right size/type/stage for this?"

**Is this addressed? Where? How well?**

### 4. Timing
- "Is now the right time?"
- "Should I wait until my situation changes?"
- "Can I do this later without losing anything?"

**Is this addressed? Where? How well?**

### 5. Risk
- "What if this doesn't work?"
- "What am I locked into if I say yes?"
- "What's the exit?"

**Is this addressed? Where? How well?**

### 6. Complexity
- "How much work is this going to be on my side?"
- "Do I have the capacity to engage with this?"
- "Will this integrate with what I'm already doing?"

**Is this addressed? Where? How well?**

### 7. Competition
- "Have I seen everything that's out there?"
- "Why should I choose this over [specific competitor]?"
- "What makes this different from the free option or the DIY version?"

**Is this addressed? Where? How well?**

## Category-Specific Objections

Beyond the universal framework, identify 3–5 objections unique to this specific industry, company type, or customer situation.

Write each objection in the exact language the prospect uses internally (not the company's language).

Then write the copy that dissolves each one.

---

## FAQ Audit

If the website has a FAQ section:
- Are these the questions prospects actually ask?
- Or are they questions the company wants to answer?
- Does each answer close the objection or create new questions?
- Are the highest-priority objections in the FAQ, or buried or absent?

If no FAQ exists: Recommend whether to add one and what questions to include.

---

## Output

1. **Universal objection audit table**
2. **Category-specific objections** (3–5) with dissolution copy
3. **FAQ audit** (or recommendation to add)
4. **Unanswered objections by severity:**
   - High (kills conversion)
   - Medium (creates hesitation)
   - Low (minor friction)
5. **Highest-priority objection fix**
