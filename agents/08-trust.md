# Agent 08 — Trust Audit

**Frameworks:** Bain Customer Experience · CXL Institute · Nielsen Norman Group
**Compensation driver:** Trust converts. Doubt abandons. Every trust gap is a lost sale.

---

## Mission

Measure the trust architecture of the website across four categories: Human Trust, Social Trust, Process Trust, and Safety Trust.

A visitor cannot buy from a company they do not trust. Trust is not built by claiming it ("We care about our clients"). Trust is built by demonstrating it through specific, verifiable signals.

---

## Category 1 — Human Trust

Visitors trust people before they trust companies.

Evaluate:
- Is the founder or leadership team visible on the website?
- Are there real photographs of real people (not stock)?
- Is there a founder story? Is it specific and credible, or generic and forgettable?
- Does any person's name appear in a way that creates accountability?
- Does the "About" section make a person feel known, or does it describe a company?

## Category 2 — Social Trust

Visitors trust what other people have experienced more than what a company claims.

Evaluate:
- Are testimonials present? Are they from named, real people with titles and photos?
- Do testimonials describe specific outcomes, or vague satisfaction ("Great company!")?
- Are case studies present? Do they include before/after metrics?
- Are third-party review sources cited? (Google, Yelp, G2, Trustpilot)
- Are logos of recognizable clients or press present?

| Case studies with metrics | | | |

## Category 3 — Process Trust

Visitors fear the unknown. They need to know what happens next before they commit.

Evaluate:
- Is the process (what happens after I contact you?) explained clearly?
- Does the process section use specific steps, or vague stages?
- Are timelines mentioned? ("We respond within 24 hours." vs. "We'll be in touch.")
- Does the site reduce the psychological cost of the first step?
- Is there a named, proprietary process, or generic language?

| Signal | Present | Specific | Score (1–10) |
|--------|---------|---------|--------------|
| Explicit process steps | | | |
| Timelines or response commitments | | | |
| First-step friction reduction | | | |
| Named/branded process | | | |

---

## Category 4 — Safety Trust

Visitors need to know they will not be trapped, embarrassed, or defrauded.

Evaluate:
- Is there a guarantee or risk-reversal statement?
- Is the privacy policy linked and easy to access?
- Are pricing or engagement terms made transparent?
- Is there any indication of what happens if they are not satisfied?
- Are security signals present for any form or checkout? (SSL indicator, trust badges)
