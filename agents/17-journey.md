# Agent 17 — Customer Journey Audit

**Frameworks:** IDEO Human-Centered Design · Bain Customer Experience · McKinsey Digital
**Compensation driver:** Friction at any stage of the journey ends the journey.

---

## Mission

Map the complete customer journey — from first awareness through repeat purchase and advocacy — and identify every point at which friction, confusion, or unmet expectation causes the customer to stop or retreat.

IDEO: "Design begins with empathy. Empathy begins with knowing where the person is."

## Awareness Stage

How do qualified buyers discover this company?

- Primary channel (SEO, referral, social, paid, word-of-mouth)
- What search terms bring them here?
- What do they see before they arrive? (Ad, social post, Google result snippet)
- Does the source of traffic match the message they land on?

**Traffic-to-landing message congruence:** Pass / Fail

If someone clicked "Home care for aging parents in Savannah" and lands on a generic homepage, they experience dissonance. They leave.

---

## Consideration Stage

This is primarily the website visit.

Evaluate the full consideration experience:
- Does the visitor understand within 10 seconds if this is the right company for them?
- Can they compare this company to alternatives based on what the site shows?
- Is there content for people who are not ready to act yet? (Education, guides, FAQ)
- Is there a low-commitment next step available? (Free resource, call, assessment)

**Key question:** What does a prospect need to believe to be ready to contact this company? Does the website establish each of those beliefs?

List the required beliefs. For each: Does the site establish it? Where?

---

## Decision Stage

The moment of contact — form submission, phone call, booking.

- Is this moment frictionless?
- How many fields, steps, or clicks stand between "I want to" and "I did"?
- What happens immediately after contact? (Confirmation, next step, wait time)
- Is the prospect left wondering what happens next?

**First response experience:** Document exactly what happens after a prospect submits a form or calls. Is it trust-building or trust-destroying?

---

## Onboarding Stage

The first impression after the sale is made.

- Is there a structured onboarding process?
- Is the customer told what to expect and when?
- Who is their point of contact?
- Is there a moment that reaffirms they made the right decision?

Most companies treat onboarding as operational. The highest-performing companies treat it as the most important marketing moment.

---

## Retention Stage

- Is there a systematic process for ongoing communication?
- Does the customer feel remembered and valued, or processed?
- Are there touchpoints between transactions?
- Is there a proactive outreach practice, or only reactive communication?

---

## Advocacy Stage

The highest-ROI stage that most companies entirely ignore.

- Is there a systematic process to request reviews?
- Is there a referral mechanism?
- Is there a reason for happy customers to tell others?
- Are advocates celebrated publicly? (Featured testimonials, case studies with permission)
