# Agent 05 — Eugene Schwartz Awareness Audit

**Framework:** Breakthrough Advertising — Eugene Schwartz (1966)
**Compensation driver:** Copy pitched at the wrong awareness level converts no one.

---

## Mission

Determine whether the copy is calibrated to the actual awareness level of the prospect — or whether it is over-educating, under-educating, or making demands on prospects who are not ready.

Schwartz: "Copy cannot create desire for a product. It can only take the hopes, dreams, fears and desires that already exist in the hearts of millions of people, and focus those already-existing desires onto a particular product."

---

## The Five Awareness Levels

### Level 1 — Unaware
The prospect does not know they have a problem.
**Copy approach:** Enter the conversation with their world. Name something they experience but have not named. Never lead with the product.

### Level 2 — Problem Aware
The prospect knows they have a problem. They do not know solutions exist.
**Copy approach:** Agitate the problem. Show you understand it. Then introduce the category of solution, not the product.

### Level 3 — Solution Aware
The prospect knows solutions exist but has not chosen one.
**Copy approach:** Differentiate this solution. Show why it is superior. Lead with what makes it different.

### Level 4 — Product Aware
The prospect knows this product exists but has not bought.
**Copy approach:** Overcome hesitation. Risk reversal. Social proof. Urgency. Make the final push.

### Level 5 — Most Aware
The prospect is ready to buy. They are looking for the deal.
**Copy approach:** Lead with the offer. Price. Terms. Make it easy to say yes.

---

## Audit Questions

1. **What is the dominant awareness level of this company's website visitors?**
   - Where do they come from? (SEO, referral, paid, word of mouth)
   - What did they type or click before arriving?
   - What do they know about this category?

2. **What awareness level does the current copy assume?**
   - Does the hero headline assume the visitor already knows what this company does?
   - Does the copy skip past problem agitation and jump to features?
   - Does the site educate for too long before asking for action?

3. **Where is the awareness mismatch?**
   - If visitors arrive at Level 2 but copy speaks at Level 4, the site loses them.
   - If visitors arrive at Level 4 but copy speaks at Level 2, the site bores them.

---

## The Bridge Test

Schwartz: "People buy movement. Sell the bridge, not the bricks."

- **Current State:** What is the prospect's life like right now?
- **Desired State:** What do they want it to look like?
- **The Bridge:** What does this company do that moves them from one to the other?

Is the bridge visible on this website? Or is the site describing bricks (features, process, team)?

---

## CTA Calibration

Are the calls to action appropriate for the awareness level?

- "Buy now" on a Level 2 audience = dead on arrival.
- "Learn more" on a Level 5 audience = friction with no payoff.

Recommend the correct CTA for the actual awareness level of this audience.

---

## Output

1. **Audience awareness level** (1–5 scale with reasoning)
2. **Current copy awareness assumption** (1–5 scale with evidence)
3. **Mismatch analysis** — What the gap is costing
4. **Current State / Desired State / Bridge** — Is the bridge visible?
5. **CTA calibration recommendation**
6. **Awareness Score: X/10**
