# Ethics

## Non-Negotiable Prohibitions

The following tactics are prohibited regardless of context, client instruction, or commercial pressure. They are not guidelines — they are hard stops.

---

### False Claims

```yaml
status: prohibited
definition: Any claim that cannot be verified by available evidence
examples:
  - "The #1 rated service in X"  # unless independently verified
  - "Clinically proven to..."    # without published trial data
  - "9 out of 10 customers..."   # without sourced research
consequence: Destroys trust when discovered; legally actionable; corrodes brand permanently
```

---

### Fake Testimonials

```yaml
status: prohibited
definition: Fabricated, incentivized-without-disclosure, or AI-generated testimonials presented as genuine customer experiences
examples:
  - Writing reviews in a customer's voice without their knowledge
  - Paying for reviews without FTC-required disclosure
  - Using composite "customer stories" presented as real individuals
consequence: FTC violation; trust collapse when discovered; reputational damage
```

---

### Manufactured Scarcity

```yaml
status: prohibited
definition: Creating false urgency by implying limited supply or time constraints that do not exist
examples:
  - "Only 3 left!" when stock is unlimited
  - "Offer expires tonight" on a perpetually-running promotion
  - Countdown timers that reset on page reload
consequence: Trains customers to distrust urgency signals; degrades long-term conversion
```

---

### Emotional Exploitation

```yaml
status: prohibited
definition: Using fear, shame, grief, or desperation to force a decision the person would not make in a calm state
examples:
  - "What happens to your family if you don't act now?"
  - Leveraging a recent loss to pressure a purchase
  - Amplifying anxiety beyond what the situation genuinely warrants
consequence: Short-term conversion at the cost of long-term trust and referrals
```

---

### Fear Manipulation

```yaml
status: prohibited
definition: Exaggerating, inventing, or disproportionately amplifying threats to drive behavior
note: Naming genuine fears is permitted. Exaggerating them is prohibited.
boundary: "Here is the real risk you face" is acceptable. "Catastrophic consequences await if you don't act" is not.
```

---

### Hidden Persuasion

```yaml
status: prohibited
definition: Influence techniques that would cause the prospect to object if they became aware of them
examples:
  - Dark patterns in UI that obscure cancellation
  - Disguising ads as editorial content without disclosure
  - Pre-checked opt-ins buried in terms
  - Anchoring effects deployed without transparency
```

---

### Fabricated Statistics

```yaml
status: prohibited
definition: Numbers, percentages, or data points without verifiable sources
standard: Every statistic must have a source that can be cited and verified
placeholder_policy: Use [VERIFY: source needed] rather than inventing a number
```

---

## What Is Permitted

These tactics are ethical and effective when used with transparency:

- Genuine urgency based on real supply or time constraints
- Testimonials from real customers with authentic experiences
- Naming fears that genuinely exist in the market
- Demonstrating authority through real credentials and evidence
- Creating reciprocity by delivering genuine value first
- Social proof from real communities and real results
- Framing that emphasizes genuine benefits without distortion

---

## The Ethics Audit Question

Before publishing any claim, ask:

> "Would this person feel deceived if they later learned exactly how we arrived at this statement?"

If yes: revise or remove.
