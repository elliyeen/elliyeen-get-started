# Consent Framework

Every recommendation produced by this system must pass all five checks before approval.

---

## The Five Checks

### 1. Understanding

> Can people understand what is being offered?

**Pass criteria:**
- The offer is described in plain language
- The value proposition is clear without industry jargon
- A person unfamiliar with the category could describe the offer after reading it
- Terms and conditions do not contradict the main promise

**Fail indicators:**
- Vague benefits ("transform your life")
- Hidden terms that modify the core offer
- Technical language that obscures cost or commitment

---

### 2. Transparency

> Are important facts visible?

**Pass criteria:**
- Price or pricing method is accessible before commitment
- Material limitations are disclosed (geographic restrictions, eligibility requirements, etc.)
- Conflicts of interest are disclosed
- Paid endorsements are labeled as such

**Fail indicators:**
- Key information buried in fine print
- Pricing revealed only after significant time investment
- Affiliate relationships undisclosed
- Sponsored content without clear disclosure

---

### 3. Freedom

> Can people decline without punishment?

**Pass criteria:**
- Opting out is as easy as opting in
- Declining does not result in social pressure, repeated solicitation, or consequences
- Cancellation process is as simple as signup
- No guilt-inducing language attached to the exit path ("Are you sure you want to abandon your family's safety?")

**Fail indicators:**
- Cancellation requires phone call when signup was one click
- Exit flows using shame or fear to reverse the decision
- Unsubscribe links that are broken or require account login
- "Soft opt-out" that still results in contact

---

### 4. Alignment

> Does this serve the interests of both parties?

**Pass criteria:**
- The buyer's desired outcome is genuinely achievable with this offer
- The seller's gain does not depend on the buyer's continued confusion
- Success for the buyer leads to success for the seller (referrals, renewals)
- The agent would recommend this offer to someone they personally care about

**Fail indicators:**
- Business model depends on switching costs or confusion
- Seller benefits from buyer not understanding the full picture
- Churn is financially preferable to retention for the seller

---

### 5. Long-Term Trust

> Will trust increase after the transaction?

**Pass criteria:**
- The experience matches the promise
- Post-purchase communication is useful, not extractive
- Problems are acknowledged and resolved, not deflected
- The customer would describe their experience as "they did what they said they would do"

**Fail indicators:**
- Onboarding undersells the product relative to the marketing
- Post-sale communication is primarily upsell-focused
- Complaints are routed to friction-heavy support flows
- The product cannot withstand an honest public review

---

## Applying the Framework

Run each recommendation through all five checks in order. A single failure disqualifies the recommendation until revised.

```
Recommendation: [describe recommendation]

Understanding:  PASS / FAIL — [note]
Transparency:   PASS / FAIL — [note]
Freedom:        PASS / FAIL — [note]
Alignment:      PASS / FAIL — [note]
Long-Term Trust: PASS / FAIL — [note]

Result: APPROVED / REJECTED — [revision required if rejected]
```

---

## The Master Question

Before final approval:

> "If the prospect understood everything we are doing, would they still willingly choose to participate?"

**Yes** → Approved.

**No** → Rejected. Revise until the answer is yes.
