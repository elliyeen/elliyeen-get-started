# First Principles of Human Behavior

Understanding these patterns is not about exploiting them. It is about aligning offers with how humans actually function — so the right people find the right solution with less friction.

---

## People Desire

These are the core motivators present in virtually every purchase, belief adoption, and behavior change.

| Desire | Description |
|---|---|
| **Safety** | Protection from harm, risk, uncertainty |
| **Freedom** | Autonomy, independence, self-determination |
| **Belonging** | Connection to people, groups, identity |
| **Status** | Recognition, respect, social standing |
| **Meaning** | Purpose, contribution, significance |
| **Security** | Financial stability, predictability, continuity |
| **Health** | Vitality, longevity, physical wellbeing |
| **Prosperity** | Abundance, opportunity, growth |
| **Simplicity** | Ease, clarity, reduced cognitive load |
| **Identity** | Alignment with self-concept and values |

### Application

Before positioning any offer, identify which desires it genuinely serves. Only claim the desires where genuine alignment exists. Do not manufacture alignment where none exists.

---

## People Follow

These are the influence sources humans naturally defer to.

| Source | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Trusted authorities** | Reduces decision risk by borrowing others' expertise |
| **Communities** | Belonging signal; if my group does it, it must be right for me |
| **Families** | Highest-trust channel; most durable referral source |
| **Friends** | Peer validation with low social distance |
| **Social norms** | "Most people do X" activates conformity drive |
| **Aspirations** | People follow who they want to become |
| **Stories** | Narrative bypasses analytical resistance |
| **Repetition** | Familiarity is mistaken for truth (availability heuristic) |

### Application

Identify which influence sources are active in your market. Build presence inside them rather than trying to replace them.

---

## People Resist

These are the friction sources that stop voluntary participation.

| Resistance | Description |
|---|---|
| **Complexity** | Too many steps, options, or concepts |
| **Risk** | Perceived chance of loss, regret, or embarrassment |
| **Confusion** | Unclear value proposition or contradictory signals |
| **Loss** | Giving up something of current value |
| **Embarrassment** | Social exposure or judgment risk |
| **Distrust** | Inconsistency between claims and evidence |
| **Friction** | Effort required to take the next step |
| **Uncertainty** | Unknown outcomes or unclear processes |

### Application

Map every step in the buyer journey against this list. Resistance is not a signal to push harder — it is a signal to redesign.

---

## Combining the Principles

Effective growth strategy:

1. **Start with desire** — what do people already want?
2. **Identify influence channels** — where do they look for guidance?
3. **Remove resistance** — what stops them from choosing you?

Growth happens at the intersection of strong desire + trusted channel + low friction.
