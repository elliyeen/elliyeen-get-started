# Growth Tests

## Purpose

Evaluate whether growth levers are working and which dimensions of the Growth Equation need attention.

Run these tests quarterly or when growth stalls.

---

## Growth Equation Audit

```
Growth =
  Awareness
  × Trust
  × Relevance
  × Reputation
  × Distribution
  × Community
  × Advocacy
```

Score each dimension 1–5. Multiply. Find the lowest scores — they are the highest-leverage improvement opportunities.

---

## Test Suite

### TEST-GRW-001: Awareness Audit

**Question**: Does the target audience know this organization exists?

**Metrics**:
- Branded search volume (people searching the organization's name)
- Social media follower growth (new, genuine followers per period)
- Website new visitor volume
- Unprompted recall in customer interviews

**Test**:
- Ask 10 prospective customers: "When you were looking for [category], what came to mind?"
- Is this organization mentioned without prompting?

**Score**:
- 5: Organization is mentioned unprompted by 50%+ of target audience
- 3: Known among referral networks but not to the broader audience
- 1: Essentially unknown outside of current customers

---

### TEST-GRW-002: Conversion Audit

**Question**: When the right audience reaches the offer, what percentage takes the desired next step?

**Metrics**:
- Website visit → inquiry rate
- Inquiry → consultation rate
- Consultation → first engagement rate
- First engagement → retention rate

**Test**:
- Calculate each conversion rate from available data
- Compare against category benchmarks where available
- Identify the step with the highest drop-off

---

### TEST-GRW-003: Retention Audit

**Question**: Do customers stay — and do they grow their relationship over time?

**Metrics**:
- Average duration of customer relationship
- Net Revenue Retention (if applicable)
- Re-engagement rate after initial engagement
- Referral rate from existing customers

**Test**:
- What percentage of customers from 12 months ago are still active?
- What was the most common reason for ending the relationship?

---

### TEST-GRW-004: Referral Audit

**Question**: Are customers generating new customers?

**Metrics**:
- Referral source tracking: what percentage of new customers came from existing customers?
- Net Promoter Score (if surveyed): how many would recommend?
- Number of active referral sources in the past 90 days

**Test**:
- In the past quarter, how many new customers cited a personal recommendation as the reason they contacted the organization?

---

### TEST-GRW-005: Channel Efficiency Audit

**Question**: Which channels are producing qualified prospects most efficiently?

**Metrics**:
- Cost per qualified lead by channel
- Time to first contact by channel
- Conversion rate by channel
- Customer quality by channel (do referred customers stay longer than search customers?)

**Test**:
- Rank each active channel by cost per acquired customer
- Identify the channel with the best combination of quality and cost

---

### TEST-GRW-006: Friction Audit

**Question**: Where are prospects abandoning the journey — and why?

**Metrics**:
- Page exit rates on key landing pages
- Form abandonment rates
- Unanswered inquiry rate (inquiries that never received a response)
- Time from first contact to first meeting

**Test**:
- Walk through the prospect's journey as if you know nothing about the organization
- Document every friction point
- Rank by severity of impact on conversion

## Scoring Template

```
GROWTH AUDIT — [Date]

Awareness:    [1-5] — [note]
Trust:        [1-5] — [note]
Relevance:    [1-5] — [note]
Reputation:   [1-5] — [note]
Distribution: [1-5] — [note]
Community:    [1-5] — [note]
Advocacy:     [1-5] — [note]

Lowest Score: [dimension]
Recommended Priority: [action to improve lowest dimension]

Overall Growth Score: [product of all scores] / 78125
```

---

## Interpreting Results

The lowest score is always the highest priority.

A zero in any dimension collapses growth entirely. Address zeros before improving 4s.

Growth does not require perfection across all dimensions. It requires avoiding catastrophic weakness in any single dimension.
