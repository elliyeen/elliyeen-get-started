# Consistency

## Principle

Align words and actions. Say what you do. Do what you say.

---

## How It Works

Humans are wired to seek consistency — in themselves and in the organizations they trust. When a brand's behavior matches its stated values and promises, trust deepens naturally. When they diverge, trust collapses — sometimes suddenly, sometimes slowly.

Consistency builds predictability. Predictability builds trust. Trust enables voluntary participation.

---

## Dimensions of Consistency

### Message Consistency

Every channel tells the same story.

The website, the social profiles, the in-person conversation, the follow-up email — they must be coherent. Discrepancies signal disorganization, deception, or instability.

Audit checklist:
- [ ] Website and Google My Business description match
- [ ] Social media bio matches website positioning
- [ ] Sales conversation reflects website promises
- [ ] Email communication matches brand voice
- [ ] In-person experience matches digital experience

---

### Promise Consistency

Every promise made is kept — or proactively renegotiated.

Promises include:
- Explicitly stated timelines ("we respond within 24 hours")
- Implied standards ("professional, personalized care")
- Process descriptions ("we supervise the first visit")
- Pricing and terms

If a promise cannot be kept, communicate before the deadline — never after.

---

### Values Consistency

The organization behaves according to its stated values even when it costs something.

The test: Does the organization make decisions that are costly in the short term because they are aligned with stated values?

Examples:
- Declining a client who is not a good fit, even when capacity exists
- Acknowledging a mistake publicly when keeping quiet would be easier
- Refusing a tactic that would work commercially but violates stated ethics

Without costly decisions, stated values are decoration.

---

### Identity Consistency

The brand identity — visual, verbal, emotional — is stable across time.

Audiences that encounter the organization on different days, different channels, or in different contexts should have a consistent experience.

This does not mean rigid sameness. It means coherent identity — the same personality expressing itself across different contexts.

---

## The Commitment Principle

Cialdini's consistency finding: when people make a small initial commitment, they are significantly more likely to follow through on larger related commitments.

This applies in both directions:
- When the organization makes small, reliable commitments and keeps them, the audience is more willing to make larger commitments in return.
- When the organization makes large claims but fails on small ones, trust erodes faster than it would if no claim had been made.

Start with small promises. Keep them perfectly. Then grow the commitments.

---

## The Consistency Audit

Run monthly.

1. List every current promise (explicit and implicit)
2. Verify operational delivery against each promise
3. Flag any gap between stated standard and operational reality
4. Either improve operations or revise the claim — never pretend the gap doesn't exist
