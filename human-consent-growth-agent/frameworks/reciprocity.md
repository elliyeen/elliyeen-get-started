# Reciprocity

## Principle

Create genuine value before requesting value.

When you give first — without strings, without expectation — you activate one of the most powerful and universal human drives: the desire to return a favor.

---

## How It Works

Reciprocity is a social norm present in every human culture studied. When someone gives us something of genuine value, we feel a natural pull to give something back.

In marketing, this is not about manipulation. It is about demonstrating the quality of the offer before asking for anything. The prospect who downloads the guide, reads the article, or receives the free assessment already has direct evidence of the organization's quality. The decision to purchase is then an informed one.

---

## Application

### Give Before You Ask

Sequence:
1. Value delivery (guide, assessment, content, tool, introduction)
2. Trust earned through experience
3. Offer presented to an already-warm prospect

Never reverse this sequence.

---

### What Counts as Genuine Value

Genuine value is specific to the audience's real situation.

- **Specific answers** to the questions they are actually asking
- **Credentials and process documentation** that reduce their uncertainty
- **Tools** that help them solve a piece of the problem themselves
- **Introductions** to other resources that serve them (even if not the organization's offer)
- **Assessment or consultation** that gives them clarity regardless of whether they purchase

What does not count:
- Generic content that could apply to anyone
- "Value" that only exists to capture an email
- Introductory content that stops before delivering the actual answer

---

## Hopkins Application

Claude Hopkins described the sampling principle: the most powerful advertisement is the product itself. Let people experience the quality of what you do before they decide.

The Savannah Family Guide is a sample. The free care assessment is a sample. The intake conversation that treats the family with dignity before any contract is signed — that is a sample.

---

## The Reciprocity Test

Before calling any content or offer "free value," ask:
- Would the recipient find this valuable even if they never purchase anything?
- Does this answer their real question, or does it stop at the point that leads to our offer?
- Would we be comfortable if this were their only interaction with us?

If the answer to any of these is no, it is not genuine value. Revise.

---

## Ethical Boundary

Reciprocity is not a trap. Giving value does not create an obligation to buy.

The goal is informed trust, not manufactured debt. If the prospect receives genuine value and chooses not to purchase, that is a success — they are better off, and they will remember who helped them.
